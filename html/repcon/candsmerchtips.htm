Merchandising Standards for Cactus & Succulents

Display a good assortment of sizes and varieties:

Sizes

  • Most of your sales will come from 2.5" and 4" C & S. Other sizes will spice up the display.
  • Display like-sizes together: 2.5", 4", etc. Stripe flats vertically, keeping price points together.

Varieties

  • A good rule of thumb is 60% Succulents and 40% Cactus.
  • Have full flats of assortments and full flats of Special Mixes, like Donkey Tails and String of Pearls.
  • Consider shape and color of product as you arrange your table.
  • Stripe like- varieties whenever possible, this adds impact to the display and encourages the customer to purchase the larger size.
  • Include blooming varieties whenever possible.
  • Group dish gardens and specimens to create more interest.

Maintenance

A disheveled display doesn't entice the customer, and creates more work for the garden center staff. 
(They don't like that). So...

  • Shuttle trays are to be displayed lengthwise along the table.
  • Make sure all tags are facing forward.
  • Condense product as it sells through, moving pots to the front of the tray, brushing dirt away and removing debris.
  • Remove empty trays as needed, realign remaining trays, pots and dish gardens to retain a balanced, appealing display.
  • Restock as soon as possible with fresh product.
  • Ensure signage is neat, clean, correct and visible to the customer.
  • Make sure POP banner is level, centered and stable.
  • Retails should be clearly posted.
